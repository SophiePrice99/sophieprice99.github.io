Interiors: Pretty in Pink

Which blush is right for you?

With 2020 still displaying a continuation of a variety of pink shades in interior design, I thought I would share a few of my favourite and perfectly pink interiors. From candyfloss, sugar pink, to darker and more dusky tones, I am sure there will be something for everyone.

Pink Bathroom Makeover: Breaking the Loo Taboo

Adding a pink splashback or shower tiles to your bathroom is probably the easiest and most impactful way to inject some pink into your bathroom. But if you're feeling brave, why not go for a pink bath? And finish it off with a Lush 'Sex Bomb' bathbomb?

Sugared Scandi

The Scandinavian trend has dominated interior design over the past couple of years, and with this, has come a variety of variations of this minimalist trend. If you are considering adding a touch of pink in your Scandi design, it is important to maintain the neutral colourways that dominate the original trend, such as greys, whites and browns.

The Power of Pink: Radiators

Warm up your room with a pink radiator. With little effort you can transform you radiators, making them a feature in your home.

Pink Doors: Interior or Exterior?

Add a charming welcome to your home or living room with a pretty, pink entrance.

Dusky Pink

Even though this interior has quite a busy design, the dusky shade of pink brings a certain amount of calm and tranquility. Almost like you've landed in a quintessentially-British, Austen novel.
